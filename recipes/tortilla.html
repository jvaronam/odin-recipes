Description

A traditional Spanish Tortilla made with potatoes, onions and eggs. It's one of Spain's most iconic dishes, that comes with a long-standing debate about whether the authentic tortilla should include onion or not, and also about whether it should be cooked well-done or left slightly runny in the center. This recipe is for 4 people.

Ingredients

  • 0.600kg of potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 6 large eggs
  • 150 ml olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the potatoes and onion, cooking slowly until tender but not browned.
  3. Drain excess oil and transfer potatoes and onion to a bowl.
  4. Beat the eggs in another bowl, then mix in the potatoes and onion. Add salt.
  5. Return mixture to the pan and cook over medium-low heat until almost set.
  6. Place a plate over the pan, flip the tortilla, and cook the other side until golden and set.
  7. Let rest for a few minutes before serving.
